New Series: Recognizing the Lion is not King

March 27, 2020

We have all been taught throughout time that the lion is “king” of the jungle. With his large paws ready to swipe at prey. To his carnivorous canine teeth not only ready to strike fear in the heart but to devour anything in its path. Now could you imagine coming face to face, just inches away from such a beast with no place to run! Well that is exactly what Daniel faced. However, that’s the literal part of the story we all recognize and know. Why? Because it’s visual, present and captivating. However, how many of you have ever considered how Daniel got to that point? Most if they know the story, would say well he did not follow the king’s orders. That would be partially correct. Though the actual answer Daniel provides himself in the very beginning of his writings. Even before the lion pit and before his friends were put in the fiery furnace. Daniel tells us that “he purposed his heart, that he would not defile himself” (Daniel 1:8). In other words Daniel made it his intention and objective not to compromise or be untrue to His commitment to God. You see Daniel understood these primary 3 things: 1. To partake in any of earthly king Nebuchadnezzar’s pagan food, drink or activities, would be disrespectful to the real King that is and was to come. 2. That the actions of his faith and revelation given by God, would lead to the lion seeking to destroy and devour him. Yet he was not afraid. 3. That his purposed heart’s desire was to be in favor and good standing with the real King that is and was to come. In prep for Part 2 take time this week to READ: Daniel chapters 1 & 2. From Greg’s TiMP Garden: In studying and preparing for this series a lot has occurred. First, trying to get a new website/blog approach launched and the Coronavirus pandemic. The latter which is effecting families in various ways from sickness, to job loss, to financial struggles even death. As with any adversity it’s forces you to make a choice. Either one of faith or one of fear. We are experiencing a time when the hearts of men and women are being tested and revealed. My prayer is that those hearts will turn to God and recognize that He is in control that He is King!
